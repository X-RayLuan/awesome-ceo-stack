# Research Standard

## Source Hierarchy

Prefer:
1. Company filings, investor relations releases, presentations, transcripts, and official websites.
2. Exchange/regulator sources.
3. Data vendors or user-provided models/files.
4. Reputable financial press and analyst summaries for context only.

Use secondary sources to triangulate, not to replace primary evidence.

## Evidence Rules

- Cite every material claim about revenue, margins, guidance, market size, price reaction, estimates, management changes, litigation, regulation, and capital allocation.
- Include source date and publication timestamp when timing matters.
- Flag when consensus, price, or estimates are missing, stale, or user-provided.
- Keep quoted text short and only when exact wording matters.

## Calculation Rules

- Show formula or driver logic for non-trivial calculations.
- State fiscal calendar, currency, units, and adjusted/GAAP basis.
- Reconcile period comparisons: QoQ, YoY, sequential, trailing twelve months, or fiscal year.
- Avoid false precision. Use ranges when source data is uncertain.

## Writing Standard

Use the analyst note pattern:
- "What happened"
- "Why it matters"
- "What changed in the model"
- "What the market may debate"
- "What to watch next"

Keep claims falsifiable. Prefer concrete drivers over generic adjectives.

## Review Gate

Before finalizing, check:
- All tickers, company names, dates, currencies, and fiscal periods are consistent.
- No unsupported recommendation language slipped in.
- No stale market data is presented as current.
- Risk/counterargument section is real, not boilerplate.
- Final artifact says it is a draft for review when used outside internal analysis.
