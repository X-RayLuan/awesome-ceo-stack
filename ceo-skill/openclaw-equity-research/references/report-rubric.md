# Equity Research Report Rubric

## Pass Criteria
- Clear ticker, company, market, and timestamp.
- Thesis is understandable without reading the whole memo.
- Evidence table includes sources or data provenance.
- At least one bull case, base case, and bear case.
- Risks are specific and tied to the thesis.
- Monitoring points are observable.
- Research limits and stale/missing data are disclosed.
- No personalized financial advice.

## Strong Report Criteria
- Identifies variant perception: what the market may be missing.
- Separates short-term setup from long-term fundamentals.
- Includes both quantitative and qualitative evidence.
- Uses valuation assumptions instead of unsupported target prices.
- Highlights what would change the analyst's mind.
- Can be handed to an analyst or PM as a first-pass research packet.

## Common Failures
- Treating technical indicators as a full investment thesis.
- Giving BUY/SELL labels without risk and falsification.
- Using stale news as if current.
- Mixing company facts with model guesses.
- Hiding data gaps.
- Over-precise targets from weak inputs.
