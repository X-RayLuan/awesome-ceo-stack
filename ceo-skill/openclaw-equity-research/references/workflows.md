# Equity Research Workflows

## Earnings Analysis

Required inputs:
- Press release, investor presentation, prepared remarks/transcript, 10-Q/10-K or 6-K/20-F when applicable.
- Prior guidance, current guidance, consensus or user-provided estimates, prior model if available.
- Price reaction and timestamp only if sourced fresh.

Output structure:
- Headline: what changed versus expectations.
- KPI table: actual, consensus/prior guide, prior period, delta, implication.
- Guidance bridge: old guide, new guide, consensus, key drivers.
- Segment commentary: growth, margin, backlog, bookings, churn, ARPU, unit economics, or sector-specific KPIs.
- Management tone: quote sparingly; summarize themes and repeated signals.
- Model actions: exact forecast lines to update and why.

## Earnings Preview

Build:
- Setup: stock move, valuation, estimate revisions, positioning, sentiment.
- Debate: 3-5 buyside questions likely to drive reaction.
- Watchlist: KPIs, guideposts, margin items, capital allocation, product/regulatory updates.
- Scenario table: bull/base/bear print and likely debate after each.

## Model Update

Checklist:
- Roll forward periods and import actuals.
- Tie reported revenue, gross margin, EBIT/operating income, EPS, cash flow, debt, and share count.
- Reconcile adjusted metrics to GAAP where possible.
- Update drivers before outputs; avoid hardcoding plugs.
- Rebuild valuation and sensitivity tables after forecasts change.
- Document every changed assumption.

## Initiating Coverage

Minimum sections:
- Investment summary and variant perception.
- Company and business model.
- Market size, growth, cyclicality, regulation, and competitive map.
- Financial history and forecast drivers.
- Valuation framework with at least one primary method and one cross-check.
- Catalysts, risks, and what would change the thesis.
- Appendix with source list and model assumptions.

## Sector Overview

Cover:
- Value chain and profit pools.
- Demand, supply, regulation, technology, and pricing drivers.
- Peer set with revenue mix, margin profile, growth, leverage, and valuation.
- Winners/losers under key scenarios.
- Open questions requiring further diligence.

## Thesis Tracker

Maintain rows for:
- Thesis claim.
- Evidence for and against.
- Catalyst or monitor.
- Source and date.
- Confidence.
- Kill criterion.
- Next action.

## Catalyst Calendar

Required columns:
- Date or expected window.
- Company/ticker.
- Event.
- Why it matters.
- Expected metric/question.
- Source.
- Confidence.
- Prep owner or next step.

## Idea Generation

Useful screens:
- Estimate revision divergence.
- Valuation versus growth/quality.
- Margin mean reversion.
- Free cash flow inflection.
- Balance sheet stress or refinancing wall.
- Short interest plus catalyst.
- Insider/ownership change.
- Product cycle, regulation, or litigation event.

Always convert screens into a shortlist with testable theses, not a pile of tickers.
